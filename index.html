How it works

A simple flow that reduces back-and-forth and supports safer prioritization.

1) Capture intent upfront

Patients select structured options with optional free text. Follow-up prompts add context (e.g., meds → symptoms; supplies → mom/baby).

2) Support prioritization

Requests are categorized as time-sensitive vs routine using safety-first escalation logic, while preserving established emergency pathways.

3) Route to the right role

Requests can route by role (licensed vs support) and escalate for broader team awareness when thresholds are crossed.

Why it matters

Built from bedside reality—designed for operational reliability.

Reduce ambiguity

Structured capture helps teams understand what’s needed the first time, lowering interruptions and relay burden.

Support equitable access

Multi-language and accessibility-focused design supports patients who are hesitant to ask for help or face communication barriers.

Improve prioritization

Time-sensitive requests are easier to see and act on, with conservative escalation designed to avoid false alarms.

Enable learning

Request logs and response patterns can support unit-level insights (e.g., common needs, bottlenecks, response timing).

Room access can be gated (e.g., signed QR links and room activation windows) to reduce inappropriate access.
